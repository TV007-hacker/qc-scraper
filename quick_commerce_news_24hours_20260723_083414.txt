FOLLOW US FOLLOW US Added to Saved Stories in Login Shares of Eternal jumped as much as 4.5% on the BSE today to hit an intraday high of ₹296.15 after brokerages issued positive commentary on the foodtech major’s Q1 FY27 performance and reiterated their bullish outlook. The stock later pared some gains to trade 3.5% higher at ₹293.35 apiece at 11:30 IST, valuing the company at ₹2.83 Lakh Cr (about $29.3 Bn). The rally followed Eternal’s June-quarter earnings, which reflected strong momentum across both Zomato’s food delivery business and Blinkit’s quick commerce operations. Brokerages highlighted Blinkit’s robust growth, improving profitability, and Eternal’s confidence in navigating rising competition. JPMorgan maintained its ‘Overweight’ rating and ₹390 target, citing strong growth across Blinkit, food delivery and District. It also pointed to Blinkit’s improving margins, while noting that higher capex per store could weigh on costs. Jefferies retained its ‘Buy’ rating with a ₹415 target, saying Eternal continues to prioritise profitable growth over aggressive discount-led market share gains. CLSA set a target price of ₹506 on Eternal, citing improving profitability of Zomato and Blinkit. It added that District and Bistro continue to broaden the ecosystem and drive engagement. Nomura also maintained its ‘Buy’ rating and ₹350 target, highlighting faster food delivery growth and improving Blinkit profitability. Meanwhile, Citi retained its ‘Accumulate’ rating and raised Eternal’s target price to ₹385 from ₹360, saying Blinkit’s strong competitive position should help it withstand discount-led competition. Eternal’s Q1 Performance Eternal posted a consolidated net profit of ₹92 Cr in Q1 FY27, up nearly 3.7X from ₹25 Cr a year earlier, though profit fell 47% from the previous quarter. Revenue rose 182% YoY and 17% QoQ to ₹20,211 Cr, largely because Blinkit now records the full value of goods sold under its inventory-led model rather than only commissions. Blinkit remained the group’s biggest revenue contributor, with revenue rising nearly 7X YoY to ₹15,664 Cr. Its net order value grew 86% to ₹17,132 Cr, while operating profit increased by ₹100 Cr sequentially to ₹365 Cr. The quick commerce business added 200 stores during the quarter, taking its network to 2,443. Inventory losses, including expiry, damage and pilferage, stood at about 1.8% of NOV. Zomato’s food delivery revenue rose 37% YoY to ₹3,100 Cr, while operating profit reached ₹621 Cr. Notably, competition has intensified with the entry of Swiggy-backed Toing and Rapido’s Ownly, though Eternal founder Deepinder Goyal said their impact on the business remained limited. “There’s no new use case being unlocked here. The customer traction is purely price driven, and price-driven traction without structural economics tends to resolve itself,” he said. Eternal is instead betting on Bistro to target lower-value food orders, while redesigning kitchens, automation and supply chains to improve speed and consistency. The company also plans to keep investing in Blinkit. CFO Akshant Goyal said Eternal had spent about ₹3,000 Cr over the past four years on stores and warehouses and would continue doing so as long as returns remained healthy. In a post-earnings call, Eternal CEO Albinder Dhindsa said that the discount war in the quick commerce segment seems to be approaching its end as there is little room for competitors to increase discounts without significantly worsening their losses. Elsewhere, Hyperpure reported revenue of ₹1,034 Cr and an operating profit of ₹14 Cr. District’s revenue rose 54% YoY to ₹318 Cr, though its loss widened to ₹61 Cr. District’s NOV increased 60% YoY to ₹3,218 Cr. Goyal said the growth was not simply driven by the IPL season, but by higher engagement across the platform. The ‘Others’ segment, which includes Bistro and AI support platform Nugget, reported revenue of ₹95 Cr, while its loss doubled to ₹90 Cr due mainly to higher R&D spending. Eternal’s board also approved the transfer of Nugget to wholly owned subsidiary Carthero Technologies through a slump sale valued at ₹35 Cr. FOLLOW US FOLLOW US Added to Saved Stories in Login Eternal’s quick commerce arm Blinkit’s adjusted EBITDA rose to ₹102 Cr in Q1 FY27 from ₹37 Cr in the preceding March quarter, marking its fifth consecutive quarter of improvement as the quick commerce platform continued to scale its operations. The vertical’s adjusted revenue grew 18.4% sequentially to ₹15,664 Cr from ₹13,232 Cr in Q4 FY26, while its net order value (NOV) increased 19% QoQ to ₹17,132 Cr. The growth came as Blinkit continued to expand its dark store network, adding 200 net new stores during the quarter to take its total count to 2,443 across the country. Reiterating its long-term strategy, Blinkit said it will continue investing aggressively in its store network and supply chain infrastructure, calling the investments its “biggest differentiator”. As part of this strategy, Eternal revised its steady-state assumptions for Blinkit’s business. It increased the estimated capex per store, including warehousing, to ₹2.5 Cr from ₹1 Cr earlier, citing larger store sizes, wider product assortment and higher technology investments in warehousing. At the same time, the company said Blinkit’s net working capital requirement has improved to 12 days of NOV from 18 days earlier. Eternal said its continued investments in working capital and capex are backed by expectations of healthy returns on capital employed (ROCE). On the product front, Blinkit said it is continuing to expand its assortment across the top eight cities while deepening its presence in the next 30 cities. It is also betting on premiumisation through the rollout of “gourmet” stores in select markets, offering curated premium and imported brands across categories. The company also shared an update on the economics of its inventory-led business model. A year after transitioning to the first-party (1P) inventory structure, Blinkit said inventory losses currently stand at about 1.8% of NOV, including expiry, shrinkage, damage, pilferage and loss in transit. Management described the figure as “a great start”, noting that perishables such as fruits and vegetables account for a significant share of the business and that it expects inventory losses to reduce further with improvements in replenishment planning and supply chain efficiencies. At the consolidated level, Blinkit parent Eternal reported a 3.7X jump in net profit to ₹92 Cr in Q1 FY27 from ₹25 Cr in the year-ago quarter. However, profit declined 47% sequentially from ₹174 Cr. Operating revenue surged 182% YoY and 17% QoQ to ₹20,211 Cr during the quarter, primarily due to Blinkit’s inventory-led accounting model. Eternal’s ‘Others’ Segment Focus The adjusted EBITDA loss of Eternal’s ‘Others’ segment, which includes AI platform Nugget, quick food delivery service Bistro, and other new initiatives, widened to ₹94 Cr in Q1 FY27 from ₹45 Cr a year ago and ₹64 Cr in the previous quarter. The company attributed the increase in losses primarily to higher R&D investments in Nugget as it scales the AI platform’s product capabilities. Nugget is Eternal’s AI-native enterprise software platform that automates customer support and business workflows using AI agents. Originally developed as an internal tool for Zomato, Blinkit and Hyperpure, it was opened to external enterprise customers in February 2025. Speaking about the investment, Eternal CFO Akshant Goyal said, “Think of this segment as the cost of experimentation — finding potential new businesses for Eternal beyond the four that exist today. We’ll invest meaningfully but with discipline.” Separately, Eternal approved an internal restructuring under which Nugget will be transferred to its wholly owned subsidiary, Carthero Technologies Pvt Ltd (CTPL). The company is also consolidating its community initiatives, including the Blinkit Ambulance Service and Feeding India, under a wholly owned Section 8 (not for profit) subsidiary, Eternal General Services Foundation. Shares of Eternal ended today’s trading session 1.15% lower at ₹283.40 apiece on the BSE.
